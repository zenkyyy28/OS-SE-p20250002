--- Orphan and Zombie Cleanup Notes ---
Q: How are orphans cleaned up?
A: Orphans are adopted by init/systemd (PID 1), which periodically calls wait() to clean them up.

Q: How are zombies cleaned up?
A: Zombies are cleaned up when the parent process calls wait() to read the child's exit status, or when the parent exits and init adopts and reaps them.

Q: Can you kill a zombie with 'kill -9'? Why or why not?
A: No. A zombie process is already dead (it has terminated). kill -9 only affects running processes. The zombie is just a process table entry waiting to be reaped.
